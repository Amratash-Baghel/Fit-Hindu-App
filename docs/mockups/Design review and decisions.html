The direction

Black, saffron, and gold — a temple at dusk, not a gym at noon

Most fitness apps read cold and clinical; most devotional apps read busy and ornamental. We wanted neither. The app should feel like an evening ritual — warm dark ground, one confident accent colour, and gold spent sparingly enough that it still feels valuable when it appears.

01

One accent, used with discipline

Saffron is the only colour that does "action" — buttons, selection, active tabs. Gold is reserved for exactly two things: the primary button and the streak. Nothing else gets to be gold — that's what keeps it feeling premium instead of festive.

02

Hindi is the voice, English whispers

Every screen leads in Hindi at full size; English sits underneath in small, muted type — a translation for anyone who needs it, never the loudest thing on screen.

03

Devotion is a feeling, not a filter

No political or partisan imagery — the warmth comes from ritual details: a shloka on the home screen, a diya‑counted streak, temple bells in the sleep sounds. Culturally warm, never divisive.

04

One system, two moods, same rules

Fitness and daytime screens stay warm‑black. Sleep sounds shifts to a calm night‑indigo — same components, same spacing, a different hour of the day. It's still unmistakably the same app.

What we need from you

Please confirm or correct these before we lock the design system

1
Overall direction — approve black / saffron / gold?

Once approved, these exact colours, type sizes and spacing become the only ones used anywhere in the app.

2
Gold used only for the primary button + streak — agreed?

We kept gold rare on purpose. If you want it to appear more often (e.g. all headings), tell us — it changes the feel a lot.

3
Devotional details — shloka, diya streak, deity chip, temple bells — comfortable with this level of devotional detail?

This is deliberately warm and cultural, not political — see any concerns here specifically.

4
Sleep screen's colour shift to night‑indigo — keep it, or make sleep sounds share the same warm‑black as the rest of the app?

Our recommendation is to keep the shift — it reinforces "this is bedtime" — but it is the one place we deviate from one palette.

5
Avatar placement — is a large video card at the top of the workout screen the right showcase for our custom avatar?

We used a placeholder silhouette. Please share a still or clip of the real avatar so we can re-check this screen against it.
